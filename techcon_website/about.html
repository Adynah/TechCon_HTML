History

TechCon is Africa’s leading multi-platform technology conference that brings together innovators, regulators, entrepreneurs, and industry leaders focused on emerging technologies like blockchain, AI, and fintech. While the specific founding date or early history isn’t detailed publicly, TechCon has grown into a key event aimed at accelerating digital transformation across Africa.

Over the years, it has evolved into a major hub for:

  • Facilitating collaboration between governments, startups, investors, and tech experts
  • Showcasing practical applications of new tech across sectors like finance, government, and education
  • Engaging policymakers with industry leaders to shape effective regulation and innovation-friendly frameworks

Mission

TechCon’s mission is to accelerate Africa’s digital transformation by creating a platform where stakeholders from various industries can learn, collaborate, and innovate using emerging technologies.

Past Speakers

Yele Bademosi

Yele Bademosi is a Nigerian entrepreneur and investor, known for his work in the cryptocurrency space. He is the CEO and founding partner of Bundle Africa, a social payments app that aims to drive the mass adoption of digital assets in Africa. Prior to founding Bundle, Yele was a director at Binance Labs, a global cryptocurrency platform. He is also the founder of Microtraction, an investment vehicle that has funded several high-profile startups in Nigeria.

Marius Reitz

Marius Reitz is the General Manager for Africa Operations at Luno, a global cryptocurrency company. With a background in finance, Marius has deepened his knowledge of cryptocurrency and has written numerous publications on trends and advocacy for cryptocurrency adoption across the continent.

Fejiro Hanu Agbodje

Fejiro Hanu Agbodje is the founder and CEO of Patricia Technologies Limited, a technology-driven alternative payments company based in Lagos, Nigeria. Founded in 2017, Patricia allows users to transact with digital currencies like Bitcoin and Perfect Money. Fejiro is also known for launching Africa’s first luxury Bitcoin debit card, enabling users to withdraw cash directly from their Bitcoin or Naira wallets.
